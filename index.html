Research

Deep technical work at the intersection of systems and language, where understanding inductive bias matters more than model size.

Tamil Classical Text Restoration System

Character-level spacing restoration for Sangam literature and ancient Tamil inscriptions - 20× better than seq2seq baselines, fully deterministic, CPU-native.

The Problem

Classical Tamil texts (Sangam literature, ancient inscriptions) suffer from spacing degradation - words merge together when spaces are lost over centuries of manuscript copying and digitization. Existing approaches using seq2seq models (ByT5) achieved only 11.95% CER with structural failures: repetition loops, hallucination, and character corruption.

The Insight

Reframed the problem from sequence-to-sequence generation to character-level insertion tagging. Instead of generating the entire output text, the model only needs to decide at each character position: insert a space here, or don't. This eliminates repetition loops and hallucination by design.

Architecture

  • CharConvTagger - 12-layer dilated 1D CNN (~15M parameters, CPU-native inference)
  • Tamil grapheme-constrained decoding - Unicode validity enforced as a hard constraint
  • Vocabulary-expanding domain adaptation for handling diverse text domains
  • Deterministic n-best reranking (no LLM, fully interpretable)

Results

0.59% CER - a 20× improvement over the ByT5 baseline (11.95%). Repetition loops and hallucination eliminated entirely. Runs on CPU with no GPU required. Fully deterministic and interpretable.
